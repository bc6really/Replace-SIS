RFP.1.2 Purpose

The purpose of this Request for Proposal (RFP) is to provide sufficient information to qualified and interested vendors enabling them to prepare and submit information regarding an Student Information System (SIS). Currently, IIRP uses a custom built solution and is looking to migrate to a more robust SIS. The SIS, NetStride (currently Sharpe Innovations), has been in use for more than 20 years. This system has been the hub of all student data. We are looking to fortify our business continuity and increase our capabilities, minimizing customization.

In addition, IIRP needs to reduce time spent on data entry and navigating screens. International Institute for Restorative Practices is going to accomplish this through leveraging the strengths of the SIS and it's integration and interoperability with Salesforce. IIRP perceives these strengths to be reduced duplication of data entry, improved integration, and end user reporting.

Lastly, we want to allow for vendors to display their methodology and practices. Because we are coming from an custom-built application that has been fully customized to the business of International Institute for Restorative Practices, we want the vendor's solution to highlight the benefits of their proposed business practices and their software that supports those practices.

RFP.2.0 Institutional Objectives

As mentioned earlier in the section 1.2, International Institute for Restorative Practices is looking to change our business application strategy from a custom SIS to a standardized solution.

Key factors for moving to an SIS solution include:
  • Implement a strong integration and flow with Salesforce
  • Deliver event based notifications to key personnel
  • Remove repetitive data entry
  • Implement a hosted or SaaS SIS solution

RFP.4.2 Implementation Services

Please provide a detailed description of your implementation services for the proposed software. Include at a minimum, the following information:

  • Implementation approach - Flash Cut-over/Phased
  • Implementation timeline
  • Risk management - Proposed solutions to implementation timeline slippage, scope creep.
  • Change control - What are areas in which implementation services do/don't explicitly cover.
  • Project organization and staffing requirements for vendor and institution, including man-hour estimates.
  • Training methodology and its relationship with the timeline.
  • Suggested training and ongoing support the for institution's technical staff and end-users. Also, include any staff restructuring suggestions that could benefit IIRP.
  • Implementation support services, including delivery and installation, legacy data conversion, customization support, consulting support etc.
  • Include sample of proposed staff from the vendor as well as proposed International Institute for Restorative Practices staffing requirements.
  • Documentation information, including technical documentation, as well as formats available (video, text, etc.)
  • Supported implementation for using a public cloud provider (such as Amazon AWS or Microsoft Azure)
